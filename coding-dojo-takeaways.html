Coding Dojo Takeaways

This is a (non-exhaustive) list of takeaways after one of our dojos:

  1. There has to be one product manager that makes decisions about the ambiguities of the Kata requirements.
  2. We need to be more disciplined. We need to have more structure for the Dojo and make sure that the structure (parts, participant roles, breaks, ...) is communicated at the beginning.
  3. We follow a BYODoSC policy: Bring Your Own Device or Stop Complaining about IDE, operating system and/or keyboard settings.
  4. Focus on baby steps all the time. If you take a baby step and it works before your time is over then you pass control to the next one.
  5. 10 participants seems to be the maximum for group dynamics.
  6. We did not listen enough to what others have to say.
  7. Everyone needs to be able to understand the direction we are going during the whole dojo.
  8. Do not start coding if the idea is not clear.
  9. Take a break in the middle of the dojo to revisit the design.
If you want to add/remove points from the list please discuss at our Google Group: http://groups.google.com/group/scala-vienna.
